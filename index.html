Goals (2nd semester)

Coming off a hard-fought first semester in which I passed with a surplux of one (1) credit, I wanted my second to be less stressful by choosing better subjects and actually working a bit on the mandatory ones, in particular grammar. With regards to my spoken word, I wanted to speak more clearly and get rid of my slurring, as well as get rid of invasive words.

Self-assesment

While at the time of me writing this I cannot yet judge my hard work, I must unfortunately report I have not made much progress on my slurring and invasive words. Although I can be understood, I've not yet gotten to a point of clarity which some of my classmates have reached. I do feel confident in my ability to hold a conversation, however.

Goals (3rd semester)

Say, it's been a bit of a rough year. Well, if I am to be honest, the first semester was still the worst. They say we learn by mistakes, and boy did I make mistakes back then. But that's neither here nor there. I've been tasked with uploading the portfolio and reflecting. Again. Well, I think I've made progress on the slurring. I just have a habit of trying to sound American when British is so much clearer. My next goal? Learn how to not run out of things to say. Or something.

Goals (4th semester)

Oh boy, it’s that time of the year again. Gotta do this again and come up with some vague arbitrary goal to apparently fix. And look, I did fix my slurring. I still pronounce things in this weird combination of British and Texan, but it’s a bit more comprehensible. My goal this semester? Get into a better state of mind. Took me a while to notice that I was really in the „zone“ last semester when I had the most work I ever had. I have a little bit less this semester, and it’s harder to get into the mindset where I’m just concentrating on my work.
